Although a sequential optimization generally is effective, it often is not efficient because it adjusts only one factor at a time. A more efficient algorithm adjusts all factors at the same time, moving in the direction that provides the greatest change (steepest ascent) in the response.

The controls on the right are identical to those on the previous tab with the exception that the optimization path is always superimposed on either a contour plot (in 2D) or a surface plot (in 3D). The direction of the steepest ascent is evaluated by comparing the response in eight directions: to the north, to the east, to the south, to the west, to the northeast, to the southeast, to the southwest, and to the northwest The four response surfaces are identical to those on the previous tab.
